What is pomodoro technique?

Pomodoro technique is a type of working way to work focused during a period of time and then taking a small rest. Every cycle is called a "Pomodoro" and after 4 Pomodoros it is time for a long rest and repeat.

Pomodoro was thought to be used with tasks (one task a number of Pomodoros), but some tasks take more than a cycle of 4 Pomodoros or can't be measured correctly.

The way I found myself easier to work around it more easly is to use the timer freely. I mean by that is that define tasks in a To do list and select one (just one not multitasking) work on it and start the pomodoto timer. Take all the necesary Pomodoros until you finish the task selected. And after finished, advance the timer to the next rest

Another important key to do successful Pomodoros and archieve a nice and healthy productivity is to make a good Do list. To make a good tasks list is to get a task and divide them into smaller tasks that are an important part of the whole task Eg. Make dinner:

  • Chop all the ingredients, take them out of the freezer, get all cookware needed, etc...

  • Cook the dinner.

In conclusion, By doing all the things above allows to reduce the procrastination, therefore be more productive and work healthier. Remember that working discipline is the key to the success.
